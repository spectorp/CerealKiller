Hi, I'm Perry Spector, and I built Cereal Killer in three weeks as an Insight Data Science Fellow. Several people in my life have severe food allergies, and it's tedious for them to manually sift through packaged foods at the grocery store to find ones that are safe for them. I used computer vision methods to detect cereal boxes in an image and identify safe ones.


Under the hood:

Data: Training an object detection model requires annotated images. Rather than manually identifying cereal boxes in images, I generated thousands of artificial images of cereal boxes superimposed on images of grocery store shelves, like the one below. Although I only used a few images of each cereal box, I increased the diversity of box images by warping, blurring, and modifying the color. I compiled cereal and allergy information in a MySQL database.

Algorithm: I trained the YOLO v3 model to detect the locations of cereal boxes in images using transfer learning. For each detected box, I use the Keras-OCR model to identify text. Then I match the box to a cereal in the database and retrieve the allergy information.

Backend: The project is coded in Python and leverages BeautifulSoup, numpy, PIL, pandas, and sqlalchemy.

Frontend: Built using Flask and matplotlib. Deployed on AWS.
